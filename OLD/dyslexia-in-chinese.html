Dyslexia In Chinese

Chinese is the only surviving language among the 100 kinds of ideograms - Chinese characters have the property of visual-spatial processing. When you read in English, the main area you tend to focus on reading is the left hemisphere area of ​​the brain, but Chinese reading also utilizes the same area but also heavily recruits the visual area of ​​the right hemisphere.

For students with dyslexia in Chinese, learning Chinese can be a struggle. There are some areas one can have difficulty with and they are namely:

  • Getting confused with visually similar words
  • Having problem with pronounciation or writing of Chinese
  • Inverting words or parts of a Chinese character
  • Laterally mirror-inverting a Chinese character
  • Having sentence errors in the composition
  • Having transcription errors when copying the blackboard

We've added dyslexia Chinese programme suitable for young children to middle schoolers with suspected dyslexia tendencies. There are three levels: Beginner, Intermediate and Advanced. Our service target group are children with low Chinese proficiency and children who do not like Chinese very much.

Our programmes focus on effective learning objectives that will help learners retain the shape of each word better using mult-sensory methods of teaching with evidences of working well with dyslexia Chinese. To help them recognize words we tend to explain how words are formed using Semantic Radical Cards.

Focused on effective learning objectives, our curriculum helps learners focus on reading, writing, listening and speaking. Our ultimate goal is to help a student with dyslexia learn Chinese more efficiently and effectively. The method mentioned above is a fraction of a multitude of ways a trained Chinese teacher in helping students with dyslexia can help.

There is also a SEN dyslexia screening test to find out where students are having problems. Individual sessions can be offered, or group classes of two to six audience members. ( According to the present situation, they can be done in form of Zoom.)
